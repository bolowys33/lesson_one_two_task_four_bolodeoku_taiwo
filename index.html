FOOTBALL FEDERATIONS

Football federations are organizations that govern and regulate the sport of football. They are responsible for organizing and overseeing football competitions at the national and international level, developing and enforcing rules and regulations, and promoting the sport globally. Football federations typically have a hierarchical structure, with national federations overseeing football at the national level, regional level such as CAF, AFC and international federations such as FIFA (Fédération Internationale de Football Association) governing football at the global level.

Global football Federation - FIFA (Fédération Internationale de Football Association)

FIFA (Fédération Internationale de Football Association) is the international governing body of association football (soccer). It was founded in 1904 and has its headquarters in Zurich, Switzerland. FIFA is responsible for organizing and overseeing international competitions such as the FIFA World Cup, which is held every four years and is one of the most-watched sporting events in the world. FIFA is also responsible for developing and enforcing the rules and regulations of the sport, promoting and developing football globally, and managing the transfer of players between clubs and countries. The organization consists of 211 national football associations from around the world and operates with the mission of improving the game of football, promoting social responsibility and sustainability, and building a better future through football.

Regional Football Federations

Regional football federations are organizations that govern and regulate the sport of football at a regional or continental level. They are responsible for organizing and overseeing football competitions within their respective regions, developing and enforcing rules and regulations specific to their regions, and promoting the sport within their regions. Regional football federations typically have a hierarchical structure, with national federations overseeing football at the national level and regional federations operating under them. Some examples of regional football federations include:

AFC (Asian Football Confederation)

AFC is responsible for organizing and overseeing football competitions in Asia, including the AFC Champions League and the AFC Asian Cup.

CAF (Confederation of African Football)

CAF is responsible for organizing and overseeing football competitions in Africa, including the Africa Cup of Nations.

CONMEBOL (South American Football Confederation)

CONMEBOL is responsible for organizing and overseeing football competitions in South America, including the Copa Libertadores and the Copa America.

UEFA (Union of European Football Associations)

UEFA is responsible for organizing and overseeing football competitions in Europe, including the UEFA Champions League, the UEFA Europa League, the UEFA Europa Conference League and the UEFA European Championship (also known as the Euro).

All these bodies have subsidiary but they are all regulated by FIFA
